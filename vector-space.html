벡터 공간

벡터 공간 공리

모든 $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w} \in V$와 모든 스칼라 $k, l$에 대해 다음을 만족하는, 덧셈과 스칼라곱이 정의된 공집합이 아닌 집합 $V$를 벡터 공간, $V$의 원소를 벡터라고 한다.

  1. $$\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v} \in V$$
  2. $$\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{u}$$
  3. $$\mathbf{u}+(\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w})=(\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v})+\mathbf{w}$$
  4. $$\exists \mathbf{0} \in V \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \mathbf{u}+\mathbf{0}=\mathbf{u}$$
  5. $$\exists -\mathbf{u} \in V \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \mathbf{u}+(-\mathbf{u})=\mathbf{0}$$
  6. $$ k\mathbf{u} \in V$$
  7. $$k(\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{v})=k\mathbf{u}+k\mathbf{v}$$
  8. $$(k+l)\mathbf{u}=k\mathbf{u}+l\mathbf{u}$$
  9. $$k(l\mathbf{u})=(kl)\mathbf{u}$$
  10. $$1\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{u}$$

벡터 공간의 성질

$\mathbf{u}$가 벡터 공간 $V$의 벡터이고 $k$가 스칼라일 때, $$0\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{0} \tag{\href{#property-1-proof}{\text{증명}}}$$ $$k\mathbf{0}=\mathbf{0} \tag{\href{#property-2-proof}{\text{증명}}}$$ $$(-1)\mathbf{u}=-\mathbf{u} \tag{\href{#property-3-proof}{\text{증명}}}$$

증명

$$0\mathbf{u}\href{#axiom-4}{=}0\mathbf{u}+\mathbf{0}$$ $$\phantom{0\mathbf{u}}\href{#axiom-5}{=}0\mathbf{u}+[0\mathbf{u}+(-0\mathbf{u})]$$ $$\phantom{0\mathbf{u}}\href{#axiom-3}{=}[0\mathbf{u}+0\mathbf{u}]+(-0\mathbf{u})$$ $$\phantom{0\mathbf{u}}\href{#axiom-8}{=}[0+0]\mathbf{u}+(-0\mathbf{u})$$ $$\phantom{0\mathbf{u}}=0\mathbf{u}+(-0\mathbf{u})$$ $$\phantom{0\mathbf{u}}\href{#axiom-5}{=}\mathbf{0}$$

증명

$$k\mathbf{0}\href{#axiom-4}{=}k\mathbf{0}+\mathbf{0}$$ $$\phantom{k\mathbf{0}}\href{#axiom-5}{=}k\mathbf{0}+[k\mathbf{0}+(-k\mathbf{0})]$$ $$\phantom{k\mathbf{0}}\href{#axiom-3}{=}[k\mathbf{0}+k\mathbf{0}]+(-k\mathbf{0})$$ $$\phantom{k\mathbf{0}}\href{#axiom-7}{=}k[\mathbf{0}+\mathbf{0}]+(-k\mathbf{0})$$ $$\phantom{k\mathbf{0}}\href{#axiom-4}{=}k\mathbf{0}+(-k\mathbf{0})$$ $$\phantom{k\mathbf{0}}\href{#axiom-5}{=}\mathbf{0}$$

증명

$$(-1)\mathbf{u}=-\mathbf{u} \quad \href{#axiom-5}{\iff} \quad \mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{0}$$ $$\therefore$$ $$\mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}\href{#axiom-10}{=}1\mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}$$ $$\phantom{\mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}}\href{#axiom-8}{=}[1+(-1)]\mathbf{u}$$ $$\phantom{\mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}}=0\mathbf{u}$$ $$\phantom{\mathbf{u}+(-1)\mathbf{u}}\href{#property-1}{=}\mathbf{0}$$
